What I Tell My Friends (with kids, who aren't "math people" if they ask about math) I call this: The Easy to Do-Set for Life Playbook: - Count everything and anything, eventually have them put things they count into groups and count the groups - Play a modified circles and stars to help with this - Have hundreds charts and reference them when they're counting - Show them how they can skip count by 10s on the hundreds chart - Make a "Function Machine" and play - Counting, place value, multiplicative reasoning, early algebraic thinking - Keep it concrete, get reps, they'll be set - Benefits for you: no future math fights over homework - Benefits for them: a foundation to learn math to the highest level they will want If you are a "math person" reading this, yeah, there are a million other things you can do. Most of my friends are not "math people" but now their kids are.
